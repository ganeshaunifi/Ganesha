Research Lines

The four pillars of the GANESHA framework

RL1
Research Line 1

Formal Network Language and Cognitive Network Surrogates

This research line focuses on the design of a formal language of the network and on the construction of cognitive network surrogates. The objective is to provide the network with an explicit, structured representation of its own state, events, actions, constraints, and causal relations, as well as of the human agent interacting with it.

The network surrogate acts as an abstract, reasoning-enabled representation of the infrastructure and its context, enabling the system to reason about consequences, alternatives, and uncertainties before acting. This line establishes the symbolic and semantic foundations required to move beyond reactive optimization and toward intentional, context-aware network behavior.

"Establishing the symbolic and semantic foundations for intentional, context-aware network behavior."

RL2
Research Line 2

Deductive Reasoning over Network Surrogates

Building on the formal language and network surrogate developed in RL1, this research line aims at developing deductive reasoning mechanisms natively embedded within the network.

The goal is to enable the network to perform multi-step, logic-driven inference over events, states, and actions, including causal and counterfactual reasoning across time, space, and domains. This includes reasoning about "why" certain outcomes occur and "what would happen if" alternative decisions were taken.

Through deductive reasoning, the network transitions from pattern-based reaction to explicit understanding and principled decision-making.

  • Multi-step, logic-driven inference
  • Causal and counterfactual reasoning
  • Principled decision-making
RL3
Research Line 3

Deductive–Inductive Integration and Human-Interrogable Networks

This research line addresses the integration of deductive reasoning with inductive learning from data, forming a unified deductive–inductive intelligence.

Inductive models are used to learn from observations, measurements, and streaming data, while deductive reasoning constrains, guides, and explains the learning process. A key objective is to make the network directly interrogable by the human user, enabling natural language queries, contextual dialogue, and adaptive responses.

The system actively collects and incorporates additional information from the user and the environment to refine its reasoning, close knowledge gaps, and personalize decisions. This transforms the network into an interactive cognitive partner rather than a passive optimizer.

"Transforming the network into an interactive cognitive partner rather than a passive optimizer."

RL4
Research Line 4

End-to-End Theoretical Foundations and Performance Characterization

The final research line provides a rigorous theoretical understanding of the GANESHA framework end to end.

This includes the formal analysis of reasoning correctness, stability, robustness, and convergence, as well as the characterization of performance trade-offs involving accuracy, latency, scalability, and resource consumption.

New metrics are developed to assess not only traditional network performance, but also reasoning quality, explainability, and reliability under uncertainty. This line ensures that the proposed framework is not only visionary, but also analytically grounded and quantitatively measurable.

  • Formal analysis of reasoning correctness
  • Stability, robustness, and convergence
  • New metrics for reasoning quality and explainability
