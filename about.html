ABSTRACT

GANESHA proposes a fundamental rethinking of communication and computing networks, shifting them from performance-driven infrastructures to cognitive systems capable of reasoning about the world and about humans.

Rather than embedding intelligence as a purely inductive, reactive layer, GANESHA introduces a network that explicitly combines formal representation, deductive reasoning, and inductive learning to understand context, causality, and consequences of actions.

At its core, the project develops a formal language of the network and a cognitive network surrogate, providing an explicit representation of network state, events, actions, and causal relations. This surrogate enables the network to reason before acting and to support multi-hop inference across time and domains.

A key novelty of GANESHA is its ability to generate personalized and context-aware responses by continuously integrating real-time data from the environment and the user with its structured, pre-existing knowledge. This fusion allows the network to adapt its reasoning dynamically, refining conclusions as new information becomes available.

Building on this foundation, GANESHA integrates native deductive reasoning with inductive models learned from data, making the network directly interrogable by the human user through contextual dialogue and adaptive information gathering.

Finally, the project establishes a rigorous end-to-end theoretical framework to characterize correctness, stability, robustness, and performance. GANESHA lays the foundations for Networks for Humans, where the network acts as a responsible cognitive partner rather than a passive optimizer.
