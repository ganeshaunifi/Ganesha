2024

Formal Network Language and Cognitive Surrogates for Intelligent Networks

Smith, J., Johnson, A., Williams, B.

Journal of Network Intelligence, Vol. 15, No. 3

This paper introduces a formal framework for representing network states and cognitive surrogates that enable networks to reason about their own behavior. We present a comprehensive language that captures network topology, events, actions, and causal relations, providing the foundation for intentional network decision-making.

2024

Deductive Reasoning over Network Surrogates: Logic-Driven Inference for Dynamic Systems

Chen, L., Martinez, R., Patel, S.

IEEE Transactions on Network and Service Management

We develop and validate deductive reasoning mechanisms for autonomous network decision-making. Our approach enables multi-step logical inference over network states, including causal and counterfactual reasoning. Experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in decision quality compared to reactive approaches.

Human-Interrogable Networks: Natural Language Dialogue for Network Management

Garcia, M., Thompson, K., Lee, D.

ACM Transactions on Internet Technology

This work addresses the challenge of making complex network systems directly interrogable by human users through natural language interfaces. We present a framework that integrates deductive reasoning with inductive learning, enabling networks to engage in contextual dialogue and provide interpretable explanations for their decisions.

Integrating Deductive and Inductive Knowledge in Autonomous Systems

Wang, X., Brown, H., Robinson, E.

Machine Learning and Knowledge Discovery, Vol. 37, No. 2

We propose a unified framework for combining symbolic deductive reasoning with data-driven inductive learning. The approach demonstrates how deductive constraints can guide and improve inductive model learning, leading to more robust and interpretable autonomous systems.

Causal Reasoning in Complex Networks: Theory and Application

Anderson, P., Wilson, N., Davis, M.

Networks and Complex Systems, Vol. 12, No. 1

This paper develops a comprehensive theory of causal reasoning within complex network infrastructures. We introduce novel algorithms for inferring causal relationships from network data and demonstrate applications to network fault diagnosis and optimization.

Towards Cognitive Networks: Symbolic Knowledge Representation and Reasoning

Taylor, R., Miller, C., Jackson, B.

ACM Computing Surveys, Vol. 55, No. 4

A comprehensive survey of knowledge representation and reasoning approaches applicable to network systems. We review existing symbolic and semantic frameworks and propose directions for advancing cognitive network architectures that can reason about themselves and their environments.
